🚀 How We Solve Things

1

Learn Fast

When something goes wrong, we do a 15-minute post-mortem, then move on.

  • What happened? Just the facts.
  • Why? Root cause, not blame.
  • Key Lesson? One takeaway to apply forward.

We don't dwell on losses, but we don't ignore them either.

2

Find the Opportunity

What opportunity are we missing? Frame it as a future gain, not a past loss.

Instead of saying: "Our CEO is overwhelmed with admin work."

Ask: "How much more revenue could we generate if the CEO spent 100% of their time on networking, partnerships, and strategic growth instead of admin tasks?"
3

Measure the Opportunity

How much revenue are we leaving on the table right now? Calculate it specifically.

Example: CEO Time Opportunity

Current State:
• CEO spends 15 hours/week on admin tasks (scheduling, paperwork, data entry)
• That's 60 hours/month on non-revenue activities

If Reallocated to Business Development:
• 60 hours/month = 15 networking meetings or strategic partnerships
• Industry average: 1 in 5 meetings leads to a deal
• That's 3 new deals per month
• Average deal value: $5,000 in GCI

Opportunity Cost:
3 deals × $5,000 = $15,000/month in missed revenue

Annual Impact: $180,000/year we're leaving on the table
4

Propose the Solution

What specific action will capture this opportunity?

Example: "Hire a full-time Executive Assistant or Virtual Admin to handle all admin tasks (scheduling, paperwork, data entry, CRM updates). CEO focuses 100% on networking, partnerships, and business development."
5

Measure the Impact

What will we invest, and what will we gain?

Input (Cost):
• Executive Assistant salary: $4,000/month
• Benefits & tools: $500/month
• Total Investment: $4,500/month

Output (Gain):
• 3 extra deals per month = $15,000/month in GCI
• Net gain: $10,500/month
• Annual net gain: $126,000/year

ROI: 233% return (For every $1 spent, we gain $2.33)
6

Implement

Execute the plan. Ideas without action are just wishes.

7

Measure & Learn

Did we actually capture the opportunity? Use the results to find the next opportunity.

🥇 How We Prioritize Things

1

List All Ideas

Get every project, initiative, and "what if" into one place.

2

Score Each Idea

Ask three questions (answer High, Medium, or Low):

  • Lift: How much will this increase revenue or closed transactions?
  • Effort: How much time, money, or resources will it take?
  • Confidence: How sure are we this will work?
3

Do the Best Thing First

High Lift + Low Effort + High Confidence = Do it now.

Quick Scoring Guide:

High Lift: Will significantly increase closed transactions or revenue

Medium Lift: Will moderately improve results

Low Lift: Minimal or unclear impact on our North Star

Low Effort: Can be done in under 2 weeks with existing resources

Medium Effort: Takes 2-8 weeks or requires some new resources

High Effort: Takes 2+ months or significant investment

High Confidence: We have data or proven examples this works

Medium Confidence: Logical but unproven in our context

Low Confidence: Purely speculative or experimental

Idea Lift Effort Confidence Priority
Fix Zillow Sync Bug High Low High Do Now
Launch Email Campaign High Medium High Do Now
Host Open House Event Medium Medium Medium Do Next
Redesign Office Space Low High Medium Do Last
4

Review & Reassess

Priorities shift. Market conditions change. New data emerges.

  • Weekly: Review top 5 priorities and adjust scores
  • Monthly: Re-score the entire backlog
  • Always: Kill projects that no longer align with our goals

The ability to say "no" or "not now" is as important as knowing what to say "yes" to.

💭 Our Operating Philosophy

We Balance Speed with Wisdom

We don't ignore the past, but we don't live in it. When we fail, we do a fast 15-minute post-mortem to capture lessons, then we immediately pivot to solving forward. This way, we learn from every mistake without getting paralyzed by analysis.

We Think in Systems, Not Silos

Our North Star (Closed Transactions) is supported by leading indicators and protected by guardrails. When we optimize one metric, we always check: "Does this harm a guardrail? Does this actually move a leading indicator?" Short-term gains that damage client trust or team morale are not gains at all.

We Default to Action

Perfect information never comes. We gather enough data to be confident, then we act. We'd rather test a "pretty good" idea this week than wait three months for the "perfect" idea. Speed of learning beats perfect planning.

We Measure What Matters

Not everything that can be measured should be managed. We ruthlessly focus on metrics that either (1) predict our North Star, (2) protect our long-term health, or (3) help us learn faster. Everything else is noise.
